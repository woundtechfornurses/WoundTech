WHAT TYPES OF WOUNDS DO NURSES CARE FOR?

Click each box for more information!

Non-Pressure Ulcers:Open wounds from loss of surface tissue, often due to circulation issues, infection, or chronic conditions
Pressure:Localized injuries from prolonged pressure
Penetrating:Wounds from objects piercing the skin
Surgical:Intentional wounds created for medical intervention
Burn:Skin or tissue damage from heat, radiation, electricity, friction, or chemicals
Superficial:Wounds limited to skin layers, not extending to tissue or muscle
Ostomy:Surgical openings in the abdominal wall for waste elimination
